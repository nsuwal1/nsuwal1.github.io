# Research

My research combines water-resources engineering, geospatial analysis, remote sensing, environmental modeling, and explainable machine learning.

## Machine Learning for Water and Public Health

I develop machine-learning models for environmental prediction and public-health risk assessment. My work includes explainable prediction of oyster-associated norovirus outbreak risk using hydroclimatic, oceanographic, and geospatial variables.

Methods used in this research include XGBoost, LightGBM, Random Forest, artificial neural networks,logistic regression, and xAI.

## Streamflow and Drought Prediction

My work evaluates machine-learning and deep-learning methods for streamflow and drought prediction in data-scarce watersheds.

Research topics include:

- Streamflow forecasting
- Drought prediction using LSTM
- Model transferability across watersheds
- Regionalization of streamflow
- Uncertainty and model-performance evaluation

## Hydrologic and Hydraulic Modeling

I use hydrologic and hydraulic models to evaluate rainfall-runoff processes, river hydraulics, flood inundation, stormwater systems, and water-resources infrastructure.

Major tools include:

- HEC-HMS
- HEC-RAS 1D and 2D
- SWMM
- SWAT and SWAT+
- Delft3D
- MODFLOW

## GIS and Remote Sensing

I use satellite and geospatial data to study water, climate, land-surface, and coastal processes.

Tools and datasets include:

- ArcGIS Pro and QGIS
- Google Earth Engine
- Landsat
- MODIS and VIIRS
- NASA POWER
- CMEMS
- Digital elevation and land-cover datasets

## Climate Change, Hydropower, and Environmental Flows

My research evaluates climate-change impacts on hydrology, hydropower production, irrigation-water demand, reservoir operation, and environmental flows.
